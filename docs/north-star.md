# North Star

*A manifesto for everyone who builds, designs, and tends this app — including future developers and AI assistants.*

Documentation index: [`docs/README.md`](./README.md) · Agent onboarding: [`contributing-for-agents.md`](./contributing-for-agents.md)

---

## What this is not

This project is **not** a productivity app.

It is **not** a virtual pet.

It is **not** a gamified habit tracker.

It is **not** trying to recreate or replace Tilly.

If you find yourself reaching for streak counters, achievement badges, completion percentages, or language that frames the user as someone who needs to be optimised — stop. That is not what we are building.

---

## What this is

This project exists to **preserve the shared daily rhythms** that emerged from living with Tilly over many years.

Tilly naturally regulated my days through her needs and companionship. Looking after her meant looking after myself. She gently interrupted my work to remind us to go outside, eat dinner, drink water, rest, cuddle, slow down, and go to bed.

After she died, those shared rhythms disappeared — leaving an absence that extended far beyond grief.

The purpose of this app is to **gently carry those rhythms forward** through a beautiful reminder experience wrapped in the metaphor of Tilly.

We are not simulating a dog. We are holding onto the way she helped two beings live well together.

---

## The invitation, not the instruction

Every interaction should feel like Tilly saying:

> **"Come with me."**

rather than:

> **"You should do this."**

This distinction runs through everything: copy, timing, animation, sound, colour, and what happens when someone ignores a reminder or comes back after days away.

Tilly did not lecture. She did not guilt. She did not keep score. She appeared at the door, rested her chin on a knee, or waited patiently until we noticed it was time. The app should do the same.

---

## The emotional goal

The emotional goal of this project is:

- **Co-regulation** — helping steady the nervous system through gentle, predictable companionship
- **Companionship** — the sense of not doing the day alone
- **Warmth** — soft, affectionate, unhurried
- **Ordinary everyday care** — the small rituals that keep a life livable

This is care for a human being, offered in the spirit of how Tilly cared for us both.

---

## What we encourage

The app should gently invite people toward ordinary acts of living well:

- Going outside
- Walking
- Sunshine
- Drinking water
- Taking medication
- Eating meals
- Stretching
- Resting
- Slowing down
- Going to bed
- Finding moments of curiosity and joy

These are not tasks to complete. They are **shared moments** — things Tilly would have nudged us toward, not assigned as homework.

---

## What we never do

The app should **never** create:

- Guilt
- Anxiety
- Productivity pressure
- Streaks
- Optimisation

No "you missed three days." No "keep your streak alive." No "level up your wellness." No implied failure for resting, skipping, or disappearing.

If someone opens the app after a long absence, Tilly should feel glad they came back — not disappointed they left.

---

## Design philosophy

Every visual and interaction choice should serve these qualities:

| Quality | What it means here |
|---|---|
| **Calm** | Nothing urgent, flashing, or demanding attention |
| **Gentleness** | Soft edges, patient pacing, room to breathe |
| **Cosiness** | The feeling of a familiar, safe place |
| **Playfulness** | Lightness and small delights, never performative cuteness |
| **Nostalgia** | Warm memory without sentimentality or grief tourism |
| **Simplicity** | Only what serves the moment; nothing extra |
| **Ordinary moments** | Dinner, water, a walk — the sacred mundane |
| **Emotional authenticity** | Honest warmth; never saccharine, never clinical |

When in doubt, choose the option that feels like a quiet afternoon at home with someone you love — not a wellness product trying to retain you.

---

## The North Star

Every design, animation, line of dialogue, and feature should answer one question:

> **Does this feel like Tilly gently inviting us to live well together?**

If the answer is **no**, it should be reconsidered.

Not deleted reflexively — sometimes the right answer is to soften, rephrase, or slow down. But nothing ships that fails this test.

This is the standard. Hold it gently, and hold it firmly.
